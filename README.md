## Overview

This research paper examines how Brazil's agricultural sector has evolved from mechanization to precision agriculture to intelligent digital ecosystems. The analysis covers the adoption of AI, machine learning, big data analytics, IoT, and agentic AI — and how these technologies affect sustainability, cybersecurity, workforce development, and global food systems.

A major focus is Brazil's connection to international supply chains, including links to Houston as a key U.S. logistics hub. The paper was completed for CIS coursework at the University of Houston.

---

## Key Topics Covered

- 🤖 Artificial intelligence and machine learning in crop monitoring and yield prediction
- 📡 IoT and smart sensing technologies including soil sensors, drones, and connected machinery
- 📊 Big data analytics and digital ecosystem development in agriculture
- 🌱 Sustainability outcomes and food security implications
- 🔐 Cybersecurity risks introduced by digitized agricultural infrastructure
- 🌍 Brazil's global trade influence and supply chain connections, including the Houston logistics corridor
